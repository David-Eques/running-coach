# Strength & Mobility Menu

A two-day strength schedule (Tue + Thu) for a runner, plus mobility
distributed across mid-week, weekend, and daily snacks. The pattern is
**inspired by the Knees Over Toes / ATG methodology** popularized by Ben
Patrick — end-range strength, eccentric loading, posterior-chain emphasis,
tendon/ligament durability — adapted into something a runner can sustain
without burning the recovery their training depends on.

Goal: full healthspan benefits (skeletal muscle, BMD, tendon adaptation,
mobility, injury prevention) on top of the running stimulus, with neither
robbing the other.

**Not affiliated with or endorsed by ATG.** The original program is
Ben Patrick's; see [atgonlinecoaching.com](https://www.atgonlinecoaching.com/)
if you want the daily 5-day version with video coaching and progressive
loading guidance. The menu below uses generic sports-science exercise names
and is the author's own compression and adaptation — not a redistribution
of ATG's content. Start every loaded movement at bodyweight or light load
and progress when the prescribed set/rep feels crisp with full range.

## Weekly structure (default)

| Day | Strength / Mobility            | Run (typical)              |
|-----|---------------------------------|----------------------------|
| Mon | optional `mobility_daily` snack | easy run                   |
| Tue | **`kot_a`** — full session      | **NO RUN** (strength only) |
| Wed | **`mobility_midweek`** ~10 min  | quality run                |
| Thu | **`kot_b`** — full session      | **NO RUN** (strength only) |
| Fri | optional `mobility_daily` snack | easy run                   |
| Sat | optional `mobility_weekend` ~15 min | long run               |
| Sun | true rest                       | rest                       |

Two strength sessions on Tue and Thu act as recovery from running. The
other four days carry all the running mileage. Healthspan upper-body work
lives inside `kot_a` and `kot_b`, not on a separate day.

## kot_a (Tuesday) — knee-dominant + chest push

~45–60 min. **Strength-only day — no run.** Treat the lifting as the
day's training stimulus.

- Backward sled drag — 10 min  (or 15 min backward incline walks if no sled)
- Bulgarian split squat (deep range, controlled eccentric) — 3 × 8 each leg
- Romanian deadlift (full range, controlled eccentric) — 3 × 12
- Incline DB press — 3 × 8
- Chin-up — 3 × 10
- Tibialis raise (banded or against a wall) — 3 × 15
- DB external rotation (rotator-cuff health) — 3 × 8 each

## kot_b (Thursday) — hip / posterior chain + shoulder push

~45–60 min. **Strength-only day — no run.**

- Push-pull sled — 5 min
- Single-leg step-up onto a 6-inch box — 3 × 15 each leg
- Eccentric-only Nordic hamstring curl (assisted as needed) — 3 × 8
- Heels-elevated deep squat (elbows-above-knees position) — 3 × 20
- Single-arm overhead press (deep ROM) — 3 × 8 each
- Standing hip-flexor raise (cable or band) — 3 × 20 each side
- Single-leg standing calf raise (full range, off a step) — 3 × 15 each

## Mobility pools (split by equipment required)

The mobility content splits cleanly into "can do anywhere with no gear"
and "needs an incline / DB / barbell." The agent draws from the right pool
depending on where the session lands.

### `mobility_home` — bodyweight only, TV-friendly

Use for `mobility_midweek` (Wed evening), `mobility_weekend` (Sat evening),
and `mobility_daily` snacks. No equipment needed; doable on a living-room
floor while a show plays in the background.

- Couch stretch shoulders-to-wall — 2–3 × 30s each side
- Bodyweight butterfly stretch (knees toward floor) — 3 × 20
- Reverse Nordic — back toward floor, kneel position — 3 × 5

### `mobility_gym` — needs equipment, do during a gym session

Add these to the end of `kot_a` or `kot_b` if you have time. Don't put
them in evening home mobility events.

- Incline pigeon (needs an incline or slant board) — 3 × 20
- J curl with wrists below the toes (needs weight + incline) — 3 × 10
- Loaded pancake / straddle stretch (needs weight) — 3 × 10
- Seated good morning, abs to bench (needs barbell or DBs) — 3 × 10
- DB pullover below parallel (needs DB) — 3 × 10

## mobility_midweek (Wednesday evening) — ~10 min

Pick 3 from `mobility_home`. Default rotation:

- Couch stretch shoulders-to-wall — 2 × 30s each side
- Bodyweight butterfly stretch — 3 × 20
- Reverse Nordic — 3 × 5

## mobility_weekend (Saturday evening) — ~15 min

Pick 3 from `mobility_home`, longer holds. Default rotation:

- Couch stretch shoulders-to-wall — 3 × 30s each side
- Bodyweight butterfly stretch — 3 × 20
- Reverse Nordic — 3 × 5

## mobility_daily — anytime snacks

`mobility_home` pool only. Five minutes while the coffee brews, while
watching TV, in a work break. Frequency matters more than duration for
mobility. Always optional; the agent never *blocks* a day for it.

## Rules the agent must follow

- `kot_a` is pinned to **Tuesday**, `kot_b` to **Thursday**. Move only if life forces it.
- **Strength days are strength-only — no run that day.** Tuesday and Thursday have no running. The lifting is the day's stimulus.
- Never schedule `kot_a` or `kot_b` the day before a long run, on race day, or in the two days before race day.
- Race week (week 12) drops both strength sessions. Tue and Thu become easy run days.
- If a strength day immediately follows a flagged-HR-drift quality run on Wed, do the upper-body half of `kot_b` only on Thu (chin-up / press / ext rotation) and skip the leg work that day.
- `mobility_midweek`, `mobility_weekend`, and `mobility_daily` snacks are *suggestions* in the calendar event description — never their own blocking event.
- "Rest" days are rest. Don't sneak strength in.

## Notes

- Start light. "Pause at the bottom" / "controlled eccentric" / "full range"
  do more for tendon adaptation than load does, especially early. Add weight
  when 3×8 (or 3×15) feels crisp with full range.
- The 2-day frequency is a deliberate runner-friendly compression. Literature support:
  - **Strength / hypertrophy**: ~95% of 3×/wk effect at matched weekly
    volume per muscle group (Schoenfeld 2016, 2019).
  - **Tendon adaptation**: tendons respond to consistency more than
    frequency; 2×/wk is well within effective range (Bohm, Murphy).
  - **Injury prevention**: 2×/wk reduces overuse running injuries 30–50%
    (Lauersen 2014).
  - **BMD**: ~70–80% of the 3×/wk effect (Florence 2022). The running
    impact already provides bone signal; strength tops it up.
  - **Mobility**: handled by distributing the mid-week and weekend
    sessions plus daily snacks — frequency wins.
- Strength here is for joint health, BMD, tendon durability, and injury
  prevention — not race performance. The running stimulus is the headline.
  Recovery wins ties.
